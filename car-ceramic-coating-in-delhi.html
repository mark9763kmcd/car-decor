QUESTION AND ANSWERS

Q. What is 9H Ceramic Coating?

A. The 9H ceramic coating protects a car's outer surface from harm caused by outside factors. The '9H' label signifies the coating's strong resistance to scratches, as tested by the Pencil Hardness Test.

Q. How much does car ceramic coating cost?

A. Ceramic coating for cars ranges from Rs. 7,999 to Rs. 10,000+, whereas ceramic coating for bikes ranges from Rs. 2,500 to Rs. 4,000+. Also, avail of the no-cost EMI benefit on a credit card for 12 months on any ceramic coating service.

Q. Is it beneficial to apply a coating on a new car?

A. Yes! Ceramic coating adds a clear layer to safeguard the paint and create a glossy look for new and old vehicles. Discover high-quality ceramic coating services in Delhi. Get genuine ceramic coating near me in Delhi, NCR, at an unmatched price from our detailing partner, 'The Detailing Gang'.

Q. Is 9H or 10H ceramic coating superior?

A. 10H Ceramic is the strongest ceramic coating that contains 80% high-grade SiO2. It is popularly known to be 200 times stronger than steel, although it is not yet officially declared to have 100% hardness. 10H is the best option right now in terms of hardness, and if you want more shine, go for 9H.

Q. What is the duration of ceramic coating?

A. Usually, ceramic coatings can endure for approximately 5 years if properly maintained. Nevertheless, as the majority of ceramic coatings are manufactured abroad and evaluated under different conditions, the lifespan of the coating may differ based on the climate, weather, and lifestyle in our nation.

Q. Which is superior: 10H or Graphene coating?

A. Both coatings are best in their class; if you want the highest hardness, then go for 10H, while if you are looking for the highest hardness with the highest gloss, then you should go for Graphene Coating.

Q. What is the superior option: Wax or Ceramic Coating?

A. Wax and ceramic have distinct characteristics. Wax provides a temporary shiny and glowing effect, while ceramic is a durable, glossy protective layer that typically needs special rubbing to be removed.
